Zero Contracts

Contracts in Zeeka are expressed by verifying keys of Zero-Knowledge Proof circuits, instead of instructions of an imaginary virtual machine, thus they are constant in size. It doesn’t matter how big the logic of your contract is, it always takes a constant fee to upload a new one.

Transaction Compression

As Zero Contracts are expressed in zk proof circuits, their state is updated through zk proofs. A single update doesn’t necessarily need to contain a single transaction. Thousands of transaction can be compressed in a single update, yet still the update is constant in
size.

Miners vs Executors

There are two ways one can contribute to the network, either by providing consensus, or executing contracts. Consensus nodes aren’t forced to run the contracts at all, they just verify their correct execution, and Executor nodes will provide proofs of correct executions.
